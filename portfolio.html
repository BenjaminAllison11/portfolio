Data Analytics · Data Visualization
DTSC-600 Breaking into Wyoming A Tableau Sales Analytics Project

The Problem
A fictitious U.S. regional retailer had no presence in Wyoming. My task was to act as an internal analyst and build a data-driven case for whether and how the company should enter that market — from identifying the right product category to recommending which salespeople to deploy.
The Data
I worked with a multi-table Excel dataset (Regional Sales) containing nearly 8,000 sales orders, joined across four sheets: Sales Orders, Store Locations, Sales Team, and Product Categories. I connected these in Tableau via left joins and built a packaged extract (.twbx) for a stable, reproducible workbook.
Technical Elements
The workbook includes bar, line, area, stacked bar, bullet, treemap, pie, histogram, box-and-whisker, scatterplot, map, and dual-axis chart types. I used calculated fields for metrics like Profit Per Person, Running Profits, Profit Markup Percent, and Above Average Flag; LOD expressions for state-level profit aggregations; and table calculations for rankings and running totals. Three parameters enabled dashboard interactivity, and filters allowed users to drill into specific states, categories, and sales channels.

The Analysis

The story I built follows a deliberate investigative arc: Why Wyoming? With zero current stores there, I first confirmed that in-store sales channels generate nearly $4M more in profit than online, making a physical location worth pursuing. Scoping the neighbors. I filtered the dataset to Wyoming's six bordering states and confirmed consistent year-over-year profit growth in the region. Category selection. Using a dynamic Top N parameter, I identified Photo Tech, Pool Accessories, and Tech Accessories as the top three profit categories across neighboring states. After flagging South Dakota and Idaho as declining markets and removing them from the candidate pool, Tech Accessories and Outdoor Furniture emerged as the strongest options. Profit per capita vs. total profit. A scatterplot comparing profit-per-capita against total profit revealed that Tech Accessories maintains strong demand even in low-population markets — a key signal for Wyoming's sparse demographics.
The recommendation
Tech Accessories was identified as the primary category, with Jenny Hawkins and Carl Nguyen recommended as the initial sales team based on their per-order profit performance in that category.
